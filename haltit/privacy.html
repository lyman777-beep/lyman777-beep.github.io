Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

1. Our Commitment

Haltit ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how Haltit handles your information when you use our application.

2. Data Collection via Accessibility Service

Haltit does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data, screen content, or private information through the AccessibilityService API. The AccessibilityService is used solely to detect System Settings UI elements (e.g., "Force Stop" button) and simulate taps — all processing happens locally on your device and no data ever leaves your phone for this purpose.

3. Third-Party Services — Advertising (AdMob)

Haltit displays advertisements served by Google AdMob. As a third-party ad network, AdMob may collect pseudonymous identifiers, including:

  • Android Advertising ID (a resettable, anonymous identifier)
  • Device telemetry (device model, OS version, screen metrics)
  • General location data (not precise GPS)

These identifiers are used solely for ad personalization and analytics. AdMob operates under its own privacy policy, which you can review at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

4. Your Choices

You have full control over your data:

  • Reset Advertising ID: Go to device Settings → Google → Ads → Reset advertising ID.
  • Opt out of ad personalization: In the same menu, enable "Opt out of Ads Personalization".
  • Revoke permissions: You can disable the Accessibility Service for Haltit at any time in System Settings → Accessibility.

5. Data Security

Haltit does not operate any remote servers. No user data is transmitted to us or any third party by the app itself. The only external network requests are made by the AdMob SDK for ad serving, which follows Google's security and privacy standards.

6. Children's Privacy

Haltit is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.
